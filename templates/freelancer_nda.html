2. Background and Recitals

WHEREAS the Company has engaged or intends to engage the Freelancer to perform certain services as a sub-contractor (the "Services");

WHEREAS in the course of performing the Services, the Freelancer may have access to or become acquainted with confidential and proprietary information belonging to the Company and/or its clients;

WHEREAS the Company is bound by confidentiality and intellectual property obligations to its own clients, and the Freelancer's obligations under this Agreement are necessary to ensure compliance with those upstream commitments;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements set forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

3. Definition of Confidential Information

3.1 "Confidential Information" means any and all information, whether written, oral, electronic, visual, or in any other form, that is disclosed by or on behalf of the Company to the Freelancer, or that the Freelancer obtains, observes, or has access to in connection with the Services, including but not limited to:

  1. Client data, client records, and client identities;
  2. Business processes, strategies, plans, and methodologies;
  3. Technical specifications, source code, algorithms, and software architecture;
  4. API keys, credentials, passwords, and access tokens;
  5. Databases, data models, and data pipelines;
  6. Financial information, pricing, and cost structures;
  7. Marketing plans, product roadmaps, and business development strategies;
  8. Employee and contractor information;
  9. Any information designated as confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure.

3.2 Confidential Information does not include information that:

  1. Is or becomes publicly available through no fault or action of the Freelancer;
  2. Was rightfully in the Freelancer's possession prior to disclosure by the Company, as evidenced by written records;
  3. Is independently developed by the Freelancer without use of or reference to the Confidential Information; or
  4. Is disclosed to the Freelancer by a third party who is not under any obligation of confidentiality to the Company.

4. Non-Disclosure Obligations

4.1 The Freelancer shall hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence and shall not, without the prior written consent of the Company, disclose, divulge, reveal, report, publish, transfer, or use any Confidential Information for any purpose other than as strictly necessary to perform the Services.

4.2 The Freelancer shall protect the Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care that the Freelancer uses to protect their own confidential information, but in no event less than a reasonable degree of care.

4.3 The Freelancer shall not copy or reproduce any Confidential Information except as reasonably necessary for the performance of the Services.

4.4 The Freelancer shall immediately notify the Company upon becoming aware of any unauthorized disclosure, use, or loss of Confidential Information.

4.5 If the Freelancer is compelled by law, regulation, or legal process to disclose any Confidential Information, the Freelancer shall provide the Company with prompt written notice prior to such disclosure (to the extent legally permitted) and shall cooperate with the Company in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy.

5. Intellectual Property Assignment

5.1 "Work Product" means all works, inventions, discoveries, improvements, designs, code, documentation, reports, deliverables, and other materials of any kind created, conceived, developed, or produced by the Freelancer, whether alone or jointly with others, in connection with or arising out of the Services.

5.2 The Freelancer acknowledges and agrees that all Work Product shall be considered work made for hire to the fullest extent permitted by law. To the extent that any Work Product does not qualify as work made for hire, the Freelancer hereby irrevocably assigns, transfers, and conveys to the Company all right, title, and interest in and to such Work Product, including all intellectual property rights therein.

5.3 The Freelancer shall execute any documents and take any actions reasonably requested by the Company to perfect, register, or enforce the Company's rights in the Work Product.

5.4 "Background IP" means any intellectual property owned by or licensed to the Freelancer prior to the Effective Date, or developed by the Freelancer independently of the Services. The Freelancer retains all rights to their Background IP.

5.5 If any Background IP is incorporated into or necessary for the use of any Work Product, the Freelancer hereby grants to the Company a perpetual, irrevocable, worldwide, royalty-free, non-exclusive license to use, reproduce, modify, distribute, and sublicense such Background IP solely as part of or in connection with the Work Product.

5.6 The Freelancer represents and warrants that: (a) the Work Product will be original and will not infringe upon the intellectual property rights of any third party; and (b) the Freelancer has full authority to grant the rights and licenses set forth in this Section.

6. Return of Materials

6.1 Upon the earlier of: (a) the completion of the Services; (b) the termination of the Freelancer's engagement; or (c) the written request of the Company, the Freelancer shall promptly:

  1. Return to the Company all documents, files, records, media, equipment, and other tangible materials containing or relating to Confidential Information;
  2. Permanently delete and destroy all electronic copies of Confidential Information in the Freelancer's possession or control, including from personal devices, cloud storage, email accounts, and backup systems; and
  3. Provide a written certification to the Company confirming compliance with this Section.

6.2 The Freelancer shall complete the return or destruction of all materials within five (5) business days of the triggering event described in Section 6.1.

7. Non-Solicitation

7.1 During the term of the Freelancer's engagement and for a period of twelve (12) months thereafter, the Freelancer shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit, contact, or engage with any client or customer of the Company for the purpose of providing services that are the same as or substantially similar to the Services, except with the prior written consent of the Company.

7.2 This restriction applies only to clients or customers with whom the Freelancer had contact or about whom the Freelancer received Confidential Information during the course of the engagement.

8. Term

8.1 This Agreement shall become effective as of the Effective Date and shall remain in force for the duration of the Freelancer's engagement with the Company.

8.2 Notwithstanding the termination or expiration of this Agreement or the Freelancer's engagement, the obligations under Sections 3, 4, 5, and 6 (Confidential Information, Non-Disclosure, Intellectual Property, and Return of Materials) shall survive indefinitely.

8.3 The obligations under Section 7 (Non-Solicitation) shall survive for the period specified therein.

9. Remedies

9.1 The Freelancer acknowledges that any breach or threatened breach of this Agreement may cause the Company irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be an inadequate remedy.

9.2 In the event of any such breach or threatened breach, the Company shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief, specific performance, and any other equitable remedies available at law or in equity, without the necessity of proving actual damages or posting a bond or other security.

9.3 The remedies set forth in this Section are cumulative and are in addition to any other remedies available to the Company at law or in equity, including the recovery of damages.

10. Governing Law

10.1 This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Province of Alberta and the federal laws of Canada applicable therein, without regard to conflict of law principles.

10.2 The Parties hereby submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of the Province of Alberta for any legal proceedings arising out of or relating to this Agreement.

11. Dispute Resolution

11.1 In the event of any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement, the Parties shall first attempt to resolve the dispute through good faith negotiation within fourteen (14) days of written notice of the dispute.

11.2 If the dispute is not resolved through negotiation, the Parties agree to submit the dispute to mediation administered in accordance with the rules of the ADR Institute of Alberta, before resorting to arbitration or litigation.

11.3 The costs of mediation shall be shared equally between the Parties, unless the mediator directs otherwise.

11.4 Nothing in this Section shall prevent either Party from seeking injunctive or other equitable relief as described in Section 9.
